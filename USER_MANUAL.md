## Getting Started

### How to Login

1. Open the Ward Watch application in your web browser
2. You will see a login page
3. Enter your **Username** in the first box
4. Enter your **Password** in the second box
5. Click the **Login** button
6. You will see the main dashboard with different sections

### Understanding the Dashboard

After logging in, you will see the main dashboard with colorful cards. Each card takes you to a different section:

- **Overview** - See all scheduled treatments for today
- **Medications** - Schedule medication orders
- **Procedures** - Schedule medical procedures
- **Investigations** - Schedule lab tests and investigations
- **Patients** - Add and manage patient information
- **Nursing Notes** - Write notes about patient care
- **Nursing Process** - Document patient assessment and care plans
- **Vital Signs** - Record patient vital signs (temperature, blood pressure, etc.)
- **Staff** - Manage doctors and nurses
- **Departments** - Manage hospital departments
- **Treatment History** - View completed treatments
- **Master Data** - Manage lists of medications, procedures, and investigations

## Managing Patients

### How to Add a New Patient

1. Click on the **Patients** card on the dashboard
2. Click the **+ Add Patient** button at the top right
3. Fill in the patient information:
   - **IPD Number** - Enter the patient's admission number
   - **Patient Name** - Enter full name
   - **Age** - Enter patient's age
   - **Gender** - Select Male, Female, or Other
   - **Contact Number** - Enter phone number (optional)
   - **Address** - Enter home address (optional)
   - **Bed Number** - Enter bed number (required)
   - **Ward** - Enter ward name (required)
   - **Diagnosis** - Enter the diagnosis (required)
   - **Admission Date** - Select the date patient was admitted
   - **Emergency Contact** - Enter emergency contact details (optional)
   - **Doctor** - Select assigned doctor from the list (optional)
   - **Nurse** - Select assigned nurse from the list (optional)
4. Click **Add Patient** button
5. You will see a success message

### How to Edit Patient Information

1. Go to the **Patients** page
2. Find the patient you want to edit in the table
3. Click the **pencil icon** (edit button) next to the patient's name
4. Update any information you need to change
5. Click **Update Patient** button
6. You will see a success message

### How to Discharge a Patient

1. Go to the **Patients** page
2. Find the patient in the table
3. Click the **checkbox icon** (discharge button) next to the patient
4. The patient will be marked as discharged
5. Discharged patients will not appear in the active patients list

### How to View Discharged Patients

1. Go to the **Patients** page
2. At the top, click the **Show Discharged Patients** button
3. You will see all patients who have been discharged
4. Click **Show Active Patients** to go back to active patients

## Recording Vital Signs

### How to Record a Patient's Vital Signs

1. Click on the **Vital Signs** card on the dashboard
2. Click the **+ Record Vitals** button at the top right
3. Select the patient from the dropdown list
4. Enter the vital signs (you can leave blank any you don't measure):
   - **Temperature** - Enter in degrees Fahrenheit (e.g., 98.6)
   - **Blood Pressure** - Enter systolic/diastolic (e.g., 120/80)
   - **Heart Rate** - Enter beats per minute (e.g., 72)
   - **SpO2** - Enter oxygen saturation percentage (e.g., 98)
   - **Respiratory Rate** - Enter breaths per minute (e.g., 16)
5. Click **Record Vitals** button
6. You will see a success message
7. The vital signs will appear in the table

### How to View Vital Signs

1. Go to the **Vital Signs** page
2. You will see a table with all recorded vital signs
3. The table shows:
   - Patient name
   - IPD number
   - Temperature
   - Blood pressure
   - Heart rate
   - Oxygen saturation
   - Date and time recorded

## Nursing Process Documentation

### What is Nursing Process?

The Nursing Process page helps you document the complete nursing care plan for each patient. It includes:
- **Sign & Symptoms** - What you observe about the patient
- **Nurse Diagnosis** - Your assessment based on observations
- **Intervention** - What care actions you plan to take

### How to Document Nursing Process

1. Click on the **Nursing Process** card on the dashboard
2. You will see a table with all active patients
3. The patient name, IPD number, and bed number are already filled in
4. The other columns (Sign & Symptoms, Nurse Diagnosis, Intervention) start empty
5. Click the **pencil icon** (edit button) next to any patient
6. Fill in the nursing process information:
   - **Sign & Symptoms** - Describe what you observe (e.g., "Patient complains of headache, appears fatigued")
   - **Nurse Diagnosis** - Your nursing assessment (e.g., "Acute pain related to surgical procedure")
   - **Intervention** - Care actions planned (e.g., "Administer prescribed pain medication, monitor pain levels every 2 hours")
7. Click **Save Changes** button
8. You will see a success message
9. The information will be saved and displayed in the table

### How to Update Nursing Process

1. Go to the **Nursing Process** page
2. Find the patient you want to update
3. Click the **pencil icon** (edit button) next to the patient
4. Update any of the three fields
5. Click **Save Changes** button
6. The updated information will be saved

## Master Data

The Master Data section lets you manage the lists of medications, procedures, and investigations that you can use when creating treatment orders.

### How to Add a Medication

1. Click on the **Master Data** card on the dashboard
2. You will see three sections: Medications, Procedures, and Investigations
3. In the **Medications** section, click **+ Add Medication**
4. Fill in the medication details:
   - **Medication Name** - Enter the name (e.g., "Aspirin")
   - **Dosage** - Enter dosage (e.g., "100mg") - optional
   - **Form** - Enter form (e.g., "Tablet") - optional
5. Click **Add Medication** button
6. You will see a success message

### How to Add a Route of Administration

1. Go to the **Master Data** page
2. Scroll to the **Routes of Administration** section
3. Click **+ Add Route**
4. Enter the route name (e.g., "Oral", "IV", "IM")
5. Click **Add Route** button

### How to Add a Procedure

1. Go to the **Master Data** page
2. In the **Procedures** section, click **+ Add Procedure**
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Procedure Name** - Enter the name (e.g., "Blood Draw")
   - **Description** - Enter description
4. Click **Add Procedure** button

### How to Add an Investigation

1. Go to the **Master Data** page
2. In the **Investigations** section, click **+ Add Investigation**
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Investigation Name** - Enter the name (e.g., "Complete Blood Count")
   - **Description** - Enter description
   - **Normal Range** - Enter normal values (optional)
4. Click **Add Investigation** button

## Treatment Orders

### How to Schedule a Medication

1. Click on the **Medications** card on the dashboard
2. Click the **+ Schedule Medication** button
3. Fill in the order details:
   - **Patient** - Select patient from the list
   - **Medication** - Select medication from the list
   - **Route** - Select how to give the medication (Oral, IV, etc.)
   - **Scheduled Time** - Select what time to give the medication
   - **Frequency** - Enter how often (e.g., "Every 8 hours")
   - **Start Date** - Select when to start
   - **End Date** - Select when to stop (optional)
   - **Dosage Amount** - Enter amount to give (e.g., "2 tablets")
   - **Priority** - Select High, Medium, or Low
   - **Notes** - Add any special instructions (optional)
4. Click **Schedule Medication** button
5. You will see a success message

### How to Mark a Medication as Complete

1. Go to the **Medications** page
2. Find the medication order in the table
3. Click the **checkmark icon** (complete button) next to the order
4. The medication will be marked as completed with a timestamp

### How to Schedule a Procedure

1. Click on the **Procedures** card on the dashboard
2. Click the **+ Schedule Procedure** button
3. Fill in the order details:
   - **Patient** - Select patient from the list
   - **Procedure** - Select procedure from the list
   - **Scheduled Time** - Select date and time
   - **Priority** - Select High, Medium, or Low
   - **Notes** - Add any special instructions (optional)
4. Click **Schedule Procedure** button

### How to Schedule an Investigation

1. Click on the **Investigations** card on the dashboard
2. Click the **+ Schedule Investigation** button
3. Fill in the order details:
   - **Patient** - Select patient from the list
   - **Investigation** - Select investigation from the list
   - **Scheduled Time** - Select date and time
   - **Priority** - Select High, Medium, or Low
   - **Result Value** - Leave blank initially (fill in when results come back)
   - **Notes** - Add any special instructions (optional)
4. Click **Schedule Investigation** button

## Common Questions

### What if I make a mistake?

- For most items (patients, staff, notes, etc.), you can click the **pencil icon** to edit the information
- Be careful with the delete button (trash icon) - this cannot be undone
- If you delete something by accident, you will need to add it again

### Why can't I see a patient in the list?

- Check if the patient has been discharged - click **Show Discharged Patients** to see them
- Make sure you're on the correct page (Patients page)
- If you just added a patient, try refreshing the page

### How do I log out?

- Click on your name or profile icon in the top right corner
- Select **Logout** from the menu
- You will be returned to the login page

### What if I forgot my password?

- Contact your system administrator
- They will be able to reset your password for you

## Tips for Best Use

1. **Fill in all required fields** - The system will show an error if you miss required information
2. **Double-check patient information** - Always verify you selected the correct patient before saving
3. **Save your work often** - Click save/submit buttons to make sure your work is recorded
4. **Use clear language in notes** - Write notes that other staff members can easily understand
5. **Keep vital signs up to date** - Record vital signs regularly for accurate patient monitoring
